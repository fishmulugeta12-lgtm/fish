The Problem

Most epoxy failures aren't epoxy's fault.

What actually causes peeling, bubbling, and yellowing

Cured epoxy is a stable, decades-durable material. The floors that fail almost always fail at the slab — moisture vapor pushing up from underneath, surface prep that was skipped to save a day, or a coating system mismatched to the traffic it's under.

  • Delamination — coating applied over untested moisture
  • Hot-tire pickup — wrong topcoat for a heated garage floor
  • UV yellowing — interior-grade resin used outdoors
  • Blistering — no vapor barrier on a below-grade slab

Why we start with a diagnostic, not a quote

Before we tell you a number, we run an ASTM F2170 moisture test and a slab profile — checking cracking, prior coatings, and porosity. That reading determines the coating system, not the other way around.

It's the same reason a structural engineer doesn't spec a beam before seeing the load. A floor quoted without this step is a guess wearing a price tag.
